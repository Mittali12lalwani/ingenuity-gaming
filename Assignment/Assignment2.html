ATAL BIHARI VAJPAYEE

Who served three terms as a Prime Minister of India.

Here's a time line of Atal Bihari Vajpayee life:

  • 1924 - Born in Gwalior, British India.

  • 1939 - Joined Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh (RSS) in the capacity of a swayamsevak.

  • 1942 - Atal Bihari Vajpayee had his first exposure to the realm of politics when he was arrested for 23 days during the Quit Ibndia Movement.

  • 1975 - Atal Bihari Vajpayee, angered by the emergency imposed by the then PM Indira Gandhi, participated in the Total Revolution movement.

  • 1977 - he was made a Union Minister under the Morarji Desai-led Janata Party coalition government.

  • 1980 - President, BJP (1980-1986) 1984 - Loses Lok Sabha election to Madhavrao Scindia from his hometown Gwalior.

  • 1992 - Conferred Padma Vibhushan.

  • 1996 - Becomes PM, but government lasts only 13 days.

  • 1998 - NDA comes to power again, sworn in as PM for the second time.

  • 2001 - Holds Agra Summit with then Pakistan President Pervez Musharraf; talks break down.

  • 2004 - NDA loses polls.

  • 2005 - Announces his retirement from politics.

  • 2015 - Conferred Bharat Ratna.

  • 2018 - dies at the age of 93

"Among Vajpayee's projects were the National Highways Development Project and Pradhan Mantri Gram Sadak Yojana. In 2001, the Vajpayee government launched the Sarva Shiksha Abhiyan campaign, aimed at improving the quality of education in primary and secondary schools."
